Section 1: About Me & Why I’m in This Class

Hi! I’m Mingyang Wu. I’m a master student in Computer Science. I’m interested in how people consume and create media in everyday life, and how new AI tools are changing those behaviors.

I’m taking this class because I want to be more intentional about my media habits—especially the tradeoffs between productivity, attention, and creativity. I also want to better understand how media platforms and tools influence what we watch, share, and make, and how that affects learning, communication, and culture.

Outside class, I spend a lot of time building and testing tools for video generation, which makes me curious about: how media gets produced, how it gets recommended, and how people judge quality and authenticity.

Section 3: A Challenge With My Media Tools

One challenge I often run into is tool fragmentation across devices and platforms. For example, I might start reading something on my phone, take notes on a laptop, and later try to find the same reference while working on a different machine.

This creates two problems for me:

  • Context switching cost: even small interruptions (notifications, switching apps) can break focus, especially during deep work.
  • Information retrieval: bookmarks, notes, and files can become scattered, making it harder to quickly retrieve what I saw or wrote earlier.

A simple improvement I’m trying is to keep a single “source of truth” for notes (one place for links + summaries), and to limit notifications during focused creation sessions.
